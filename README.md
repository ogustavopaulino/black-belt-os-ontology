# Ontology of Organizational Viability

This repository contains the ontological foundation of the Black Belt OS™.

The Ontology of Organizational Viability addresses a distinct question:

> What must an organization continuously sustain in order to persist as an organization over time?

## Core Proposition

Organizational viability is distinct from identity, performance, legitimacy, resilience, and organizationality.

These concepts may describe what an organization is, how it is perceived, or how well it performs. Viability concerns the minimum conditions required for continued organized existence.

## Minimal Conditions of Organizational Viability

The theory identifies three irreducible conditions:

1. **Temporal Orientation**
   Present activity must be structured in relation to continued existence over time.

2. **Structured Coordination**
   Activities, roles, decisions, and resources must be organized into a coherent relational structure.

3. **Operational Enactment**
   Coordination must be converted into concrete action, production, and effects.

Because these conditions must be continuously sustained, organizational viability requires their reproduction over time.

## Organizational Reproduction

Reproduction is not mere repetition.

It is the ongoing renewal of the conditions that allow an organization to remain capable of coordinated existence and action.

The patterned organization of this reproduction constitutes a viability grammar.

## Relationship to the Black Belt OS™

The conceptual architecture is organized as follows:

**Ontology of Viability**
Defines the minimum conditions required for organizational persistence.

**Grammar 9P**
Specifies the structural processes through which those conditions are reproduced.

**Black Belt OS™**
Operationalizes the grammar as an organizational operating system.

**Black Belt Studio**
Provides the environment in which the system is instantiated, observed, and evolved.

**Glorian**
Acts as the sovereign intelligence responsible for interpreting, coordinating, and evolving the system.
